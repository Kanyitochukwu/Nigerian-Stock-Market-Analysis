# Nigerian-Stock-Market-Analysis
Finding the best investment opportunities

## Project Overview

Investing in stocks can be challenging because investors must balance two competing goals: earning strong returns while avoiding excessive risk.

In this project, I analyzed seven major Nigerian stocks across four sectors—Banking, Manufacturing, Telecommunications, and Oil & Gas—to determine which companies offered the most attractive investment opportunities during 2025.

Using Excel, MySQL, and Power BI, I transformed raw stock market data into an interactive dashboard that helps investors compare companies, understand market trends, and make data-driven decisions.


## Key Questions Answered

1. Which stocks delivered the best returns?

Not all stocks perform equally. Some consistently increase in value, while others struggle to generate returns.

This analysis identified which stocks produced the strongest average gains during the year and whether those gains justified the risks involved.

2. Which sectors performed best?

The project compared four sectors:

* Banking
* Manufacturing
* Telecommunications
* Oil & Gas

This helped determine where investors could find the strongest opportunities.

3. Does higher risk lead to higher returns?

Many investors assume that taking bigger risks automatically leads to bigger rewards.

The analysis tested whether stocks with larger price swings actually generated better returns.

4. What was overall market sentiment?

By comparing the number of stocks that gained value against those that lost value each day, I assessed whether investors were generally optimistic or cautious throughout the year.

5. Which stocks showed the strongest momentum?

Momentum analysis helped identify stocks that were consistently moving upward versus those struggling to maintain growth.

## Major Findings

Finding #1: MTN Nigeria and Dangote Cement Dominated the Market

MTN Nigeria had the highest market capitalization at ₦10.72 trillion, closely followed by Dangote Cement at ₦10.20 trillion.

Together, these two companies accounted for a significant share of the total portfolio value, making them major drivers of market performance.


Finding #2: Banking Stocks Provided Diversification

Four of the seven stocks belonged to the banking sector.

This provided investors with multiple investment options within one industry while reducing reliance on a single company.

Banking stocks generally offered stability and consistent participation in market growth.


Finding #3: Not All Large Companies Perform the Same

A large company is not automatically the best investment.

The analysis showed that investors should evaluate:

* Returns
* Volatility (price fluctuations)
* Trading activity
* Sector performance

rather than focusing only on company size.


Finding #4: Liquidity Matters

Liquidity refers to how easily investors can buy or sell a stock.

Stocks with higher trading activity are easier to trade because there are more buyers and sellers in the market.

This is particularly important for large investors who need to enter or exit positions without significantly affecting prices.

⸻

Finding #5: Data Beats Guesswork

One of the most important findings was that investment decisions should be based on measurable performance indicators rather than assumptions or market rumors.

By analyzing returns, market trends, and company characteristics, investors can make more informed decisions and better manage risk.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data analytics can transform raw stock market information into meaningful investment insights.

By analyzing 1,728 stock-price records across seven major Nigerian companies, I identified key market trends, compared sector performance, and developed an interactive Power BI dashboard that supports informed investment decisions.

The analysis highlights the importance of evaluating company size, market trends, and performance metrics together rather than relying on assumptions. It also demonstrates how modern analytics tools can help investors better understand opportunities and risks within the Nigerian stock market.
